The Red Flags: How to Spot a Reseller

Before you talk about velvet or gold foil, you need to know who you're talking to. Here's how to identify a trading company (reseller) posing as a factory:

  • Generic Samples: If they only show "stock" designs and struggle to explain how a custom die is made, they probably don't own the machinery.
  • Long Communication Loops: If you ask a technical question (e.g., "What is the GSM of the velvet liner?") and they take 24 hours to "get back to you," they're likely messaging a real factory behind the scenes.
  • No Certification: Ask for their ISO 9001 or FSC certification. A real factory will have these in their own company name. A reseller will have a mix of certificates from different sources.
Pro Tip: Ask for a live factory video tour or a "Production Line Photo" with your name written on a piece of paper in the frame. A real manufacturer like LuxoPack will do this instantly.

Jewelry-Specific Needs: Beyond the Box

Jewelry is sensitive. It's high-value, it can scratch, and it can tarnish. When sourcing custom jewelry packaging boxes, you must focus on the "functional" luxury:

1. Anti-Tarnish Linings

Standard cardboards and glues release sulfur and other gases as they "cure." For silver and 14k gold, this causes oxidation (blackening). A reputable manufacturer will use specialized anti-tarnish microfiber or velvet and low-sulfur adhesives to ensure your pieces stay bright in the box for years.

2. Secure Compartments

A jewelry box is a travel case. Whether you need ring slots, necklace hooks with a pouch, or bracelet elastics, the "insert" is critical. We recommend high-density EVA foam covered in velvet. It's soft enough to prevent scratches but firm enough to hold the weight of heavy gold chains during shipping.

3. The "Snap" Factor

In jewelry, the sound of the box matters. For magnetic boxes, the magnet grade must be N52 neodymium to provide that satisfying "snap." For traditional hinged boxes, the spring tension must be consistent across 1,000 units. This is the difference between a "cheap" box and a luxury keepsake.

Top Box Styles for Jewelry Brands

There are three main styles that dominate the luxury jewelry market today:

1. The Magnetic Rigid Box

The most popular for high-end fashion jewelry and boutiques. It's sleek, easy to stack, and the flat surfaces are perfect for gold foil logos. Best for necklaces and large earrings.

2. The Sliding Drawer (Matchbox)

A modern favorite for "minimalist" brands. Often paired with a ribbon pull. It feels more like a "gift" and is highly reusable for the customer.

3. The Two-Piece Rigid Box (Lid and Base)

Classic, timeless, and very protective. It's the standard for high-jewelry (engagement rings, fine diamonds). It provides a full "unveiling" experience when the lid is lifted.

Key Specs Checklist for Your Quote

When you contact a jewelry packaging manufacturer, copy and paste this list to get an accurate quote:

  • Internal Dimensions: e.g., 90x90x40mm (Necklace), 50x50x40mm (Ring).
  • Material: 1500g Greyboard + 157g Art Paper.
  • Lining: Anti-tarnish velvet over EVA foam insert.
  • Finishing: Soft-touch lamination + Gold Foil Stamp on lid.
  • Closure: Hidden magnetic closure (N52).
  • Quantity: e.g., 500 pcs per size.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best materials for custom jewelry boxes?

For luxury jewelry, the standard is rigid greyboard (minimum 2mm) wrapped in high-quality specialty paper or velvet. The interior should feature an anti-tarnish material like microfiber or specialized velvet with a high-density EVA foam insert to secure the jewelry during transit.

How can I tell if I'm buying from a factory or a reseller?

A real manufacturer like LuxoPack can provide detailed videos of their production lines, specific ISO 9001 and FSC certifications in their own name, and can offer much faster customization and prototyping. Resellers often have generic samples and struggle to provide technical details on materials and glues.

Can you provide anti-tarnish inserts for silver jewelry?

Yes, we source specialized anti-tarnish velvet and microfiber materials. We also use low-sulfur adhesives and specialized "curing" processes for our boxes to ensure they don't release gases that cause silver and gold to tarnish while in storage or transit.
