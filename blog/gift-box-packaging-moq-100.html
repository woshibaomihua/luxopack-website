Custom Gift Box Packaging with Low MOQ (100 pcs): Best Options for Small Brands

📅 April 3, 2026 ✍️ LuxoPack Editorial ⏱ 6 min read

One of the most common frustrations for small brands and DTC startups sourcing packaging: you find a beautiful manufacturer, then discover their minimum order quantity is 5,000 pieces. For a brand launching a new product line or testing a seasonal SKU, that's a $10,000+ inventory risk before a single sale.

The good news: custom gift box packaging with MOQ 100 pieces absolutely exists — if you know where to look and how to ask.

Why MOQ Matters More Than Price Per Piece

First-time buyers fixate on unit price. Experienced operators focus on total commitment. At $1.80/piece with 5,000 MOQ, you're committing $9,000 upfront. At $2.50/piece with 100 MOQ, you're risking $250. If the design needs iteration (and it usually does), the low-MOQ option saves you thousands.

The smart scaling strategy:
Start at 100–300 pcs → validate with real customers → reorder at 500–1,000 pcs → unlock volume pricing. Most brands land at 1,000+ pcs within 2–3 orders, saving 40–50% on unit cost vs. their first order.

What to Expect at 100 pcs: Pricing & Timeline

Be realistic about cost at low MOQ. At 100 pieces, you're paying for setup and handling amortized over fewer units. Typical pricing from a factory-direct supplier:

  • Magnetic Rigid Box (100 pcs): $1.80–$2.50/pc + $60–$150 dieline setup (often waived)
  • With soft-touch lamination + foil stamp: $2.40–$3.20/pc
  • Sample before bulk: $40–$80, refunded on first order over 300 pcs
  • Lead time: 15–18 days production + 3–5 days DHL shipping

How to Get the Best Low-MOQ Deal

1. Be specific from the first email

Factories prioritize customers who know what they want. Include: box type, approximate dimensions (L×W×H in cm), quantity, finishing (matte/gloss/soft-touch), and any logo files. Vague inquiries go to the bottom of the queue.

2. Use standard sizes where possible

Custom dimensions require new tooling, which adds cost. Ask the factory for their "standard size range" — using an existing tool saves $50–$150 and can shave 2–3 days off production.

3. Limit finishing complexity at low MOQ

At 100 pcs, stick to one primary finishing technique. Matte lamination + foil stamp is the sweet spot — low setup complexity, high visual impact. Multiple finishing layers (foil + spot UV + emboss) are best reserved for 500+ pcs where setup costs amortize.

4. Commit to a reorder verbally

Tell the factory "if the sample is approved, we plan to reorder 500 pcs within 60 days." This often unlocks better service, faster turnaround, and occasionally a dieline setup fee waiver — because they know you're a growth account, not a one-time buyer.
