How to Create an Unboxing Experience That Goes Viral: 8 Packaging Tips for Brands

📅 April 3, 2026 ✍️ LuxoPack Editorial ⏱ 7 min read

Unboxing videos have been watched over 10 billion times on YouTube. The brands that dominate these searches — Apple, Glossier, Drunk Elephant — didn't get there by accident. They engineered the unboxing moment the same way they engineered the product: deliberately, obsessively, and with a clear understanding of what triggers a customer to pull out their phone.

Here are 8 packaging tactics you can implement right now with custom rigid gift boxes — even at a 100-piece MOQ.

72% of consumers say packaging design influences their purchase decision.
(Ipsos x DS Smith, 2024)

The 8 Tactics

01

Lead With a Tactile Moment

The first 3 seconds of an unboxing determine whether the customer films it. Soft-touch lamination on a magnetic rigid box creates an immediate sensory response — customers run their fingers across it before they even open it. This pause is your brand communicating: "we thought about you." Budget alternative: matte lamination is 30% cheaper and still far superior to gloss.

02

Design the Interior as a Second Reveal

Most brands obsess over the exterior and ignore the inside. Open a LuxoPack magnetic box and you see a full-color printed interior, a velvet or foam insert holding the product in place, and sometimes a scent strip. The interior is what gets photographed. Invest $0.15–$0.25/pc more in interior printing — it's the highest-ROI packaging upgrade available.

03

Add a Handwritten-Style Thank You Note

A printed card that mimics handwriting (use a script font, not Arial) creates a personal connection at near-zero marginal cost. Print 1,000 cards for under $50. The message doesn't need to be clever — "Made for you" or "We're glad you're here" outperforms no card every time in customer satisfaction surveys.

04

Use a Satin Ribbon Pull for the Reveal

A single satin ribbon inside the box, attached to the base, creates a "curtain pull" reveal moment that customers love filming. Cost: $0.03–$0.06 per box. Impact: customers who experience ribbon pulls are 2.3x more likely to share the unboxing on Instagram stories (LuxoPack client data, 2025).

05

Create a Signature Scent

Luxury brands know: scent triggers memory like nothing else. A custom scented tissue paper inside your box — just $0.04/sheet — means every time a customer smells that scent, they think of your brand. This is next-level brand anchoring that 99% of DTC brands haven't discovered yet.

06

Make the Exterior Photograph-Ready

The box will be set on a surface before opening. Make sure it's beautiful from that angle. Gold foil logo on matte black = instant luxury. Embossed logo on deep navy = editorial quality. The rule: your box should look good in a photo taken with no styling, no props, natural window light. Test this yourself before bulk production.

07

Include a QR Code to a Private Experience

Print a QR code inside the lid linking to a 60-second "welcome video" from your founder, an exclusive discount, or a private community. This bridges physical packaging to digital engagement. Tools like Flowcode let you track scans — so you finally know the unboxing conversion rate from packaging to repeat purchase.

08

Make It Too Beautiful to Throw Away

The ultimate retention signal: a box so well-made that customers repurpose it. Jewelry boxes become storage boxes. Candle boxes become book displays. When a customer's friends ask "what's that beautiful box?", your brand earns an organic referral. Design for a second life — it costs nothing extra and creates brand ambassadors.

The 1-Page Unboxing Brief Template

Before briefing your packaging manufacturer, answer these 6 questions:

  • What emotion do you want the customer to feel at first touch? (Surprise? Luxury? Warmth? Exclusivity?)
  • What's the one thing that must be true about the exterior? (Logo visibility, specific color, texture)
  • What will the product look like inside? (Shape, weight, fragility — drives insert choice)
  • Is there anything in the box besides the product? (Card, sample, ribbon)
  • Where will this be opened? (Home? Office? In-store?) — drives light and background considerations
  • What do you want the customer to do after opening? (Share, scan QR, leave a review)
